Why Cyber Security is Important

Average Cost of Data Breach

The global average cost of a data breach has reached $4.45 million in 2023, marking a 15% increase over the last three years. In the United States, this cost is significantly higher at $9.48 million.

Detection and Escalation Costs

These costs have jumped 42% over the same period, becoming the largest portion of breach costs. This underscores the complexity and resource intensity of breach investigations.

Ransomware Recovery Costs

The average cost of ransomware recovery is nearly $2 million. This emphasizes the expensive and disruptive nature of ransomware attacks on businesses.

Ransomware Payout Increase

The average ransomware payout has increased from $812,380 in 2022 to $1,542,333 in 2023, indicating the escalating costs of such attacks.

Data Breach Lifecycle

Organizations that implement AI and automation experience a data breach lifecycle that is 108 days shorter compared to those that do not use these technologies

Phishing Attacks

Phishing remains a leading attack vector, accounting for more than 80% of reported security incidents. Every minute, $17,700 is lost due to phishing attacks

Insider Threats

About 43% of all breaches involve insider threats. These can be both intentional and unintentional but are significant contributors to security incidents.
